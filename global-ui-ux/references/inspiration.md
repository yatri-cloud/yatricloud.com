# Inspiration & Taste

Taste is built by looking at a lot of good work and understanding *why* it's good. Curated sources
plus how to actually use them.

## Where to look

### Award & showcase galleries (overall craft)
- **Awwwards** — `awwwards.com` — the benchmark for cutting-edge web craft (sometimes over-designed;
  steal the polish, not always the complexity).
- **Godly** — `godly.website` — astronomically good web design, curated.
- **Land-book** — `land-book.com` — huge landing-page gallery, filterable by style/industry.
- **Httpster** — `httpster.net` — modern, tasteful, less flashy than Awwwards.
- **Minimal.gallery** — `minimal.gallery` — for restraint-driven design.
- **SaaS Landing Page** — `saaslandingpage.com` — real SaaS marketing sites.
- **Mobbin** — `mobbin.com` — real app/web UI flows (great for patterns, not just aesthetics).
- **Refero / pageflows / screenlane** — real product screens and flows.

### Motion & interaction
- **motionsites.ai**, **Codrops** (`tympanus.net/codrops`) — interaction and animation techniques.

### Components & systems (implementation-ready)
- **21st.dev**, **shadcn/ui** (`ui.shadcn.com`), **Aceternity UI**, **Magic UI**, **Origin UI**,
  **Tailwind UI** — production-quality component patterns you can adapt.
- **Vercel**, **Linear**, **Stripe**, **Raycast** — study their marketing + app UIs; they set the
  modern bar for SaaS craft.

### Design systems to learn from (the theory)
- **Material Design 3** (`m3.material.io`) — the most thorough public system (color, type, motion, a11y).
- **Apple HIG** (`developer.apple.com/design`) — the standard for native/mobile idioms.
- **Radix / shadcn**, **Polaris** (Shopify), **Carbon** (IBM), **Atlassian**, **Primer** (GitHub) —
  real, documented, battle-tested systems.

### Color & type
- **Radix Colors**, **`uicolors.app`**, **Leonardo** (`leonardocolor.io`), **Coolors** — palettes.
- **Fontshare**, **Google Fonts**, **`fontsource.org`**, **`fontpair.co`**, **Typewolf** — fonts & pairings.
- **Realtime Colors** (`realtimecolors.com`) — preview a palette on a real layout instantly.

## How to use references (steal like an artist)

1. **Collect 3–5**, not 50. More than that and you blend them into mush.
2. **Name what works.** Don't just save a screenshot — write *why*: "the generous section spacing,"
   "the two-weight type system," "borders instead of shadows," "the single accent color." That
   sentence is the reusable insight.
3. **Extract the system, not the surface.** Copy the *approach* (opacity-driven grays, tight display
   tracking, one CTA per section), not the exact pixels of someone's brand.
4. **Combine and make it yours.** Take the spacing rhythm from one, the type idea from another, the
   color restraint from a third. Originality is a new combination of understood parts.
5. **Never ship a 1:1 clone.** It's a starting point, not the destination — and their palette/voice
   won't fit your product.

## Reading list (the canon, worth the time)
- *Refactoring UI* — Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger (the single most practical book for developers doing design).
- *Laws of UX* — Jon Yablonski (`lawsofux.com`) — the psychology, condensed.
- *Don't Make Me Think* — Steve Krug — usability fundamentals.
- *Grid Systems* — Josef Müller-Brockmann — the theory behind layout discipline.
- *Thinking with Type* — Ellen Lupton — typography fundamentals.
- **Refactoring UI tips** and **Steve Schoger's** threads — free, dense, immediately applicable.

## A note on trends
Glassmorphism, brutalism, big-blur gradients, bento grids — trends are fine to adopt, but they date
fast. The fundamentals in `principles/` and `foundations/` don't. Build on the fundamentals; season
with a trend if it fits the brand. A trendy site with broken hierarchy still looks bad; a
fundamentally sound site looks good for years.
